Korean Forced Alignment

Version: 1.7.0

Purpose

    Speech and text alignment information have been crucial for various researchers, providing insights into how written words are realized in human speech. However, manual alignment is time-consuming and often inaccurate due to varying human decisions. Automatic forced alignment, developed by researchers, overcomes these issues, offering fast and consistent alignment results.

    The Korean Forced Aligner (Korean FA) automates the alignment of Korean speech signals and their orthographic transcriptions at the word and phone levels. This aligner utilizes Kaldi to create the best-performing model by training and evaluating multiple models generated using various algorithms.

Model

    The Korean FA model was trained using the Kaldi toolkit based on a 1000-hour speech corpus recorded from diverse speaker groups encompassing different ages, regions, and sexes.

Data Preparation

  1. Audio files (.wav) (with a sampling rate of 16,000Hz)
    • Provide audio file(s) in WAV format ('.wav') with a 16,000Hz sampling rate.
      Korean FA assumes an input audio file(s) sampling rate of 16,000Hz.
    • Name your audio files following specific rules (e.g., name01.wav, name02.wav, ...).
  2. Text files (.txt)
    • Name your transcription text files with ordered numbers as suffixes, and they should be identical to your audio files as pairs (e.g., name01.txt, name02.txt, ...).
    • Each text file should contain a complete sentence written in Korean characters.
    • Do not include punctuation marks such as periods ('.') or commas (',') in the text file.
    • Remove any trailing white spaces (or tabs) at the end of lines.
  3. Recommendations for Better Performance
    • Use fewer white spaces between characters.
    • Leave spaces between words in the transcription as per the speaker's reading pace.
    • Avoid strict adherence to prescriptive spacing rules; use whitespace for pauses.
